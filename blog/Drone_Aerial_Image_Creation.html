Creating An Aerial Image From A Drone Flight for Civil Drawings

October 25, 2024
Tags:
Civil AutoCad Carlson Drone

In this recording I walked through the process of creating an aerial image using a drone. My project focused on a boat ramp in Kentucky, where we're working on improvements following a dam removal. I share how I collected and processed drone images to create both a 2D aerial photo.

I started by showcasing the final aerial image. I explain my drone flight planning process using Drone Link, detailing how I set up the drone mission, including flight paths, altitude, and speed adjustments to ensure proper photo capture.

After flying the mission with my DJI Mini 2 drone, I gathered a series of images. I used ground control points to help ensure accuracy, which I surveyed with a total station on site right before the drone flight. The next step involved processing these images with Web ODM Lightning, where I uploaded the photos and waited for the processing to complete.

Once the model was ready, I moved to AutoCAD, where I used Carlson Civil Suite to georeference the aerial image. I detailed how to input control points, scale the image accurately, and adjust it until it aligned properly with my surveyed points.

Finally, I exported the georeferenced image and demonstrated how to import it back into AutoCAD, verifying that everything lined up correctly. Overall, I hope this breakdown provides a clear understanding of the entire process from planning to execution.
